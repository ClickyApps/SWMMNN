Religious and spiritual support
The Chaplaincy Team has chaplains from the following religions: Christian (Church of England, Free Church, Roman Catholic), Muslim, Sikh, Buddhist, Hindu.
There is normally a Chaplain on-site between 8am and 4pm (weekdays) and on-call for emergencies at other times. Contact us on 0121 333 8526 or 0121 333 8527.
There are several places for quiet prayer on the first floor open 24hours a day: Christian Chapel, Muslim Prayer Room, Multi-faith Quiet Room. Services/prayers are held weekly. The Chapel also has a children’s area.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
Ward 11 is on the second floor.
From the main corridor:
By lift: turn right. The lifts are a short distance along the corridor, to your left. Turn right on coming out of the lift at the second floor. Ward 11 is towards the end of the corridor, to the right. By walking: turn left. Do remember that the floors in the main building are taller than modern floors, and the second floor is the same as the third floor in a modern building! The stair-well is about half way down the corridor, on your left. Walk up to the second floor. Ward 11 is opposite and slightly to your right on coming out of the stair-well.
Press the buzzer to enter the ward .

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nurse caring for your baby.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
Dana Oliver (Manager), Anital Patel (Deputy Manager) or nurse in charge.

Talking to the medical team
Daily ward round from 10am (Monday 10.30, Wednesday 11am).

Baby rest time
Check with staff.

What to expect when you arrive

How to access the Unit
NSW can be accessed through Ward 9 on the second floor, but the quickest way is via Loveday Street. From the main hospital entrance turn left, and then left again at the Emergency Department. Follow the side of the building and you will see the Loveday Street entrance. Neonatal Surgery is on Floor 3.
Press the buzzer to enter the ward.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care
The nurse caring for your baby or the surgical team. A neonatologist from Birmingham Women’s Hospital visits the Unit several times a week.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns
The nurse caring for your baby, the nurse in charge, ward manager or surgical team.

Talking to the medical team
Daily ward round from 8.30-9.30, but doctors visit throughout the day.

Baby rest time
1-3pm.
